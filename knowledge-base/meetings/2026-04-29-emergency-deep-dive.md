# Emergency Deep-Dive — ER/Specialty Line of Service

Special-topic deep-dive session called to advance [[2026-03-30-emergency-clinic-line-of-service]]. 4-hour working session. Larkspur partner ([[david-lansky]]) and independent director ([[dr-amelia-foster]]) attended.

## Pre-read

- Jennifer's build-side capacity + economics modeling
- James's acquire-side target identification (3 candidate networks)
- Tom's capital-deployment scenario modeling
- Elena's clinical-quality risk assessment

## Discussion

### Build case (Option B)

Jennifer presented:
- 8-12 flagship clinics convertible to 24-hour ER footprint
- $4-7M capex per clinic; $50-90M total program
- 18-24 month staffing ramp per clinic
- Margin profile: ER per-hour revenue 2x GP; staffing burden offsets significant share — net 12-15% margin steady-state, vs 18% GP

### Acquire case (Option A)

James presented:
- 3 candidate networks identified, 15-30 hospitals each
- Likely deal sizes $150-280M
- Multiple expectations 12-15x — well above Pawline's GP comfort zone
- Would require either third Larkspur round or refinancing
- Likely in-flight pipeline conversation Q3-Q4

### Defer case (Option C)

- Continue out-of-network ER referrals
- Forgo ~30% UC→ER capture revenue ([[urgent-care-er-conversion-30-percent]])

## Stakeholder positions (post-discussion)

- Sarah: now leaning Option B build, but only if Jennifer confirms operational capacity
- Elena: more open to Option B than at offsite — clinical-quality risk seems manageable with deliberate flagship-by-flagship rollout
- Jennifer: confident in operational capacity if pace is 2-3 clinics/year not parallel
- Marcus: Option B viable if ROD-team capacity is rebuilt post-dyad-rollout
- Tom: Option B fits within current capital structure; Option A requires refinancing
- James: open to either; 2 of the 3 acquire targets become less attractive if we're 12 months out

## What was decided

- Sarah, Marcus, Jennifer, and Elena to draft a Option B implementation roadmap by 2026-06-15
