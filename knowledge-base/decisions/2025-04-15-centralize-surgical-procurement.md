# Decision: Centralize surgical supply procurement

**Status.** Accepted 2025-04-15. Implementation completed 2025-09.

## Context

Through Q1 2025, every Pawline clinic procured surgical supplies independently — sutures, gowns, drapes, gloves, anesthesia disposables, surgical implants. Pricing varied 8-20% across clinics for identical SKUs. Vendor relationships were inherited from acquired clinics; some clinics held three vendor accounts for materially overlapping product categories. The Q4 2024 procurement audit ([[surgical-supply-cost-savings-12-percent]]) sized the savings at ~12% on the surgical supply category.

## Decision

Move surgical supply procurement to corporate. Designate two preferred vendors per category, negotiate volume pricing, route all clinic orders through a centralized procurement portal. Central catalog enforces SKU standardization; clinic-specific exceptions require ROD approval.

## Alternatives considered

- **Stay decentralized**: rejected. The ~$3M/year savings opportunity outweighs the autonomy concession.
- **Centralize all clinical procurement (drugs, lab, supplies, capital equipment)**: too aggressive in one step. Surgical supplies first, with the option to expand if successful.
- **Group purchasing organization (GPO) only**: explored but didn't yield the same savings as direct-vendor negotiation at our volume.

## Implementation

- Procurement portal (custom NetSuite-integrated) launched June 2025
- Phase rollout by region: Mountain (smallest) → Southwest → West
- Exception process: clinic-specific SKUs requiring approval through the ROD; exceptions tracked monthly

## Consequences

- **Realized savings**: ~12% on the surgical supply category, ~$2.7M annualized — close to the audit estimate
- **Operational disruption**: clinic ops resented the procurement portal in the first 60 days, especially at acquired clinics with longstanding vendor relationships. Post-90-day satisfaction recovered.
- **Lab consolidation followed**: 2024's [[lab-vendor-consolidation-savings]] consolidated outside labs (Antech-primary). Surgical procurement built on that pattern.
- **Future expansion**: drugs (especially controlled substances) considered for centralization in 2026-2027
