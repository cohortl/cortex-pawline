# Decision: Fixed-fee pricing pilot for preventive bundles

**Status.** Proposed 2026-04-12. Target resolution: Q2 leadership offsite follow-up call.

## Context

Pawline's pricing model is fee-for-service. Each visit, procedure, vaccine, and dispensed item is itemized. Industry chatter and competitor moves (notably Banfield's Optimum Wellness Plans) suggest a fixed-fee preventive bundle — annual wellness exam + vaccinations + parasite prevention + dental cleaning at a packaged price — drives client retention and acquisition.

[[pet-owner-demographics-shifting-younger]] showed millennial pet-owners disproportionately favor predictable pricing. [[client-acquisition-cost-rising-q1-2026]] showed CAC up 18% in Q1 2026; a stronger value proposition could ease acquisition costs.

## Proposed pilot

- 6 clinics in CA + AZ
- 12-month pilot
- Bundle: annual wellness exam, full core vaccine schedule, year-long parasite prevention, one annual dental cleaning grade-1, and 10% off ad-hoc visits
- Two pricing tiers: standard pet ($65/month) and senior pet ($95/month)
- Auto-renew with 30-day cancellation
- Restricted to a single pet per plan; multi-pet households can stack plans

## Alternatives considered

- **Stay fee-for-service**: status quo. The risk of doing nothing is gradual share loss to fixed-fee competitors.
- **Optimum Wellness clone (full Banfield-style)**: too aggressive at this scale. Banfield-style requires tight protocol uniformity that Pawline still has gaps in.
- **Annual lump-sum prepayment, not monthly**: rejected by the proposal author (Marcus). Monthly is the affordability hook.

## Stakeholder positions

- [[marcus-kim]]: proposing the pilot; sees retention upside as the dominant case
- [[dr-elena-vasquez]]: supportive in principle but worried about clinical-decision distortion (DVMs scheduling more procedures because they're "covered")
- [[dr-jennifer-park]]: skeptical, especially of the urgent-care discount component — fears margin compression
- [[tom-brennan]]: wants explicit margin modeling under various utilization assumptions before approving
- [[dr-sarah-okafor]]: open, but wants the pilot scoped tightly (the 6 clinics is partly her conservatism)

## Why this is proposed not yet accepted

- Margin modeling under various utilization assumptions still in progress
- Cornerstone billing system needs configuration changes for subscription-style billing — implementation cost and timeline unclear
- The client-services scripts and front-desk staff need training material — a real lift, especially at acquired clinics
