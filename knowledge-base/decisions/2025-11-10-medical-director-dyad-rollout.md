# Decision: Roll out the medical director dyad model across all regions

**Status.** Accepted 2025-11-10. Rollout in progress; West region ROD seat still vacant.

## Context

The dyad model — paired Regional Medical Director (DVM) and Regional Operations Director (non-clinical) — was piloted in West in 2024. The pilot's NPS uplift evidence was striking ([[nps-correlates-with-medical-director-engagement]]: clinics with engaged RMD chart-rounds posted +18 NPS points). The question by Q4 2025 was whether to standardize.

## Decision

Standardize the dyad model across all regions. Each region is led by an RMD/ROD pair with the scope and authority defined in [[medical-director-dyad]]. RMDs report to CMO (clinical); RODs report to COO (operational).

## Alternatives considered

- **Single Regional VP per region**: rejected. The role conflates clinical and operational responsibilities; either gets neglected, usually clinical.
- **RMD only, no ROD**: rejected. RMDs end up doing facilities + lease + practice-manager hiring, eroding clinical-leadership capacity.
- **Centralize regional ops at corporate, RMDs only at regional**: rejected. Regional ops needs to live regionally — the cluster economics ([[regional-cluster-economics]]) and lab logistics demand a person on the ground.

## Implementation

- West: pilot continued; original ROD departed Q1 2026, search active
- Mountain: Karen Beckett joined as ROD Q1 2025, full dyad operating since Q2
- Southwest: David Liu joined as ROD Q3 2025, full dyad since Q4

## Consequences

- **Recruiting demand**: high-quality ROD candidates are scarce; we're competing with hospital-systems and dental-services groups for the same talent profile
- **Compensation cost**: dyad model costs ~$300-450K/year in regional leadership comp incremental to what a single Regional VP would
- **NPS impact**: tracking; expecting NPS uplift to materialize Q2-Q3 2026 in Mountain and Southwest as it did in West
- **Cultural shift**: RMDs report it makes their clinical-leadership work more focused; RODs find the partnership clarifying once it's running

- The ROD role's career trajectory is less defined than the RMD's; needs work on the ladder
