# Decision: Launch telehealth pilot

**Status.** Accepted 2025-08-04. Pilot live since 2025-09 in 6 clinics.

## Context

Telehealth in vet medicine is regulated state-by-state, with varying restrictions on what can be done virtually (preventive consultations are broadly permitted; prescription decisions for new patients vary). Demand evidence existed from competitors and pet-owner surveys. The pilot question: is there demand at the volume that makes it economically interesting to Pawline, and does it cannibalize in-clinic visits or expand the pie?

## Decision

Launch a 6-clinic pilot covering CA (3 clinics), AZ (2), CO (1) — the three most pro-telehealth state regulatory environments in our footprint. Scope: triage / advice consultations for established patients, post-op follow-ups, behavioral consults, and select dermatology recheck. Excluded: prescribing for new patients, complex sick-visit diagnosis.

Metrics:
- Visit volume (conversion of in-clinic referrals to telehealth + net-new telehealth)
- Cannibalization rate (share of telehealth visits that would otherwise have been in-clinic)
- Revenue per telehealth visit
- Client satisfaction (NPS subset)

## Alternatives considered

- **Skip telehealth entirely**: rejected. Demographic shift evidence ([[pet-owner-demographics-shifting-younger]]) suggested missing this would cost us share with millennial pet-owners disproportionately.
- **National rollout, not pilot**: too risky operationally. Telehealth requires PIMS workflow changes, scheduler training, billing changes — not the kind of thing to push to 80 clinics on first attempt.
- **Vendor-platform telehealth (third-party) vs in-network**: chose in-network — Pawline DVMs treating Pawline clients, not a contract telehealth panel. Continuity matters.

## Consequences (so far)

- **Volume**: 240% YoY growth ([[telehealth-pilot-240-percent-yoy-growth]]) off a small base — promising trajectory
- **Cannibalization**: ~28% of telehealth visits would have been in-clinic — net-new ~72%
- **Doctor adoption**: uneven; Tier A flagship DVMs adopted faster than Tier B
- **Schedule integration**: bigger lift than expected; Cornerstone scheduler not designed for video-visit blocks
- **Next**: pilot extension decision in Q3 2026 — expand to all West and Southwest, or hold and study cannibalization further
