# Decision: Accept Larkspur second institutional round

**Status.** Accepted 2025-01-20. Closed 2025-03-15.

## Context

Pawline's first Larkspur round (2019) capitalized the rollup at ~50 clinics. By Q4 2024, we were at 65 clinics with the credit facility largely drawn and a strong 2024 acquisition cohort outperforming 2023's ([[acquired-cohort-2024-outperforming-2023]]). The choice: take a second round to fund the continued M&A pace, or slow down and let cash flow fund organic growth + smaller acquisitions.

## Decision

Accept Larkspur's second institutional round. Terms: additional preferred equity, dilution to existing common holders (~12%), one additional Larkspur board seat, and an extended hold horizon to ~5 years from close (i.e., target exit around 2030).

## Alternatives considered

- **Slow down and self-fund**: would have meant 4-6 acquisitions per year vs the ~12-15 run rate; would have left geographic gaps unaddressed in 2025-2026.
- **Strategic partner / minority round**: explored 2 conversations, neither offered terms competitive with Larkspur's, both wanted control rights we weren't willing to give.
- **Refinance credit facility only**: would have provided liquidity but at higher cost of capital and without growth-equity to deploy aggressively.

## Consequences

- **Dilution**: ~12% to common holders (mostly Sarah and the Larkspur first-round). Acceptable given the capital deployment opportunity.
- **Board composition change**: Larkspur added a board seat (currently the Principal seat); Pawline added the second independent director seat ([[dr-amelia-foster]] joined ~2 quarters later)
- **Hold horizon extension**: explicit 5-year hold from second close means we're optimizing for 2030 exit — informs strategic-plan ([[2026-2030-strategic-plan]]) horizon
- **Capital deployment pressure**: with capital in hand, there is genuine PE-side desire to deploy quickly. The [[2026-01-22-q3-ma-pause]] is a partial deliberate pushback on this.
