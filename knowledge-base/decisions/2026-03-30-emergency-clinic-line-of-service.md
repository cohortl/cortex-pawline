# Decision: Emergency clinic line of service — acquire freestanding ER network or build organically

**Status.** Proposed 2026-03-30. Target resolution: Q3 2026 board.

## Context

ER/specialty represents ~5% of Pawline's volume today, concentrated in flagship clinics. [[urgent-care-er-conversion-30-percent]] shows ~30% of urgent-care presentations at flagships convert to an ER referral. Currently most of those referrals leave the network — Pawline doesn't have enough ER capacity to retain them. The strategic question: should ER be a real Pawline line of service, and if so, how?

## The two options

### Option A — Acquire a freestanding ER/specialty network

- Identify a regional ER/specialty network (15-30 hospitals) within or adjacent to Pawline's footprint
- Likely deal size: $150-280M
- Requires capital beyond what's currently available; refinancing or third Larkspur round
- Multiple expectations: 12-15x adjusted EBITDA (vs Pawline's 7-9x for GP)

### Option B — Build organically inside flagship clinics

- Convert 8-12 existing flagship clinics to 24-hour ER footprint over 24 months
- $4-7M capex per clinic for build-out + equipment
- 18-24 month staffing ramp per clinic for 24-hour DVM coverage
- Total program cost: $50-90M
- Risk: operational distraction during the [[2026-01-22-q3-ma-pause]] integration recovery

### Option C — Defer / status quo

- Continue ER referrals out-of-network for the foreseeable future
- Forgo the ~30% UC→ER capture revenue ([[urgent-care-er-conversion-30-percent]])

## Stakeholder positions

- [[dr-sarah-okafor]]: leaning Option B but unconvinced; wants more data
- [[dr-elena-vasquez]]: cautious on either path; ER medicine is its own discipline and clinical-quality risk is high
- [[dr-jennifer-park]] (Southwest RMD, ER background): strong proponent of Option B; convinced the operational competence exists
- [[dr-amelia-foster]] (board): leans Option A; thinks acquisition is the disciplined path
- [[david-lansky]] (Larkspur): Option A enthusiast; sees capital-deployment opportunity; would advocate refinancing
- [[tom-brennan]]: cautious; wants to see ER unit-economics modeling before committing
- [[dr-james-okwu]]: open; has identified 2 potential Option-A targets in pre-LOI conversations

## Why this is open

The deciding inputs aren't yet in:
1. Modeling of in-network ER capture rate uplift if Option B build-out completes
2. Defensible Option-A target valuations under current multiple environment
3. Operational capacity assessment — can Pawline run a 24-hour ER footprint without compromising GP delivery?
