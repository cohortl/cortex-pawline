# Decision: Pet insurance partnership — exclusive vs multi-partner

**Status.** Open as of 2026-04-29. Target resolution: Q3 2026 board.

## Context

Pet insurance penetration in the US is rising fast — from ~3% of pets in 2018 to ~12% in 2025. Insurance dramatically changes client behavior at the visit (more diagnostics accepted, fewer payment-plan negotiations). Pawline does not have a formal partnership; clients use whatever insurer they have, and the front desk handles claims paperwork case-by-case.

Three partnership shapes are on the table:

1. **Exclusive** — partner with one insurer, with deep PIMS integration, pre-authorization workflow, faster claim payment, and a Pawline-branded plan option
2. **Preferred + multi-partner** — recommend one insurer to clients but accept all major insurers; some workflow integration
3. **Status quo** — no preferred partner, accept all insurers transactionally

## Stakeholder positions

- [[marcus-kim]]: leans option 1 (exclusive), because the integration depth is what changes the client and DVM experience
- [[dr-elena-vasquez]]: cautious about exclusive — DVMs should not be in a position where their treatment recommendations are constrained by the insurance partner's coverage rules
- [[tom-brennan]]: option 2 most likely — partnership economics aren't large enough to justify exclusivity friction
- [[dr-sarah-okafor]]: open, leaning option 2; wants to see how peer consolidators (especially Mars-owned Banfield/VCA networks) handle this
- [[david-lansky]]: pushing for resolution by Q3 board; mildly leans option 1 for the partnership-revenue line

## Top candidate insurers (researched)

- Trupanion — clinical-quality reputation, strong direct-pay model
- ASPCA / Crum & Forster — broad market presence, weaker tech integration
- Pets Best — mid-market, willing to negotiate on integration
- MetLife Pet — newer entrant, aggressive on partnership terms
- Healthy Paws — strong clinical relationships, smaller scale

## Why this is open

- Treatment-influence concern needs structured exploration before exclusive is acceptable to clinical leadership
- Integration-cost estimates from candidate insurers vary 3x; technical due diligence not complete
- Q1 2026 client-survey data on insurance-driven preferences is not yet aggregated
