# Clinical quality review

Pawline's quarterly chart-audit framework. The mechanism by which the SOC protocol library is kept honest — without audits, the library is aspirational.

## What it is

A structured chart audit conducted by the RMD (or the RMD's designee) on a stratified sample of cases at every clinic in the region. Each clinic gets reviewed at least quarterly; flagship clinics get reviewed every 6 weeks.

## What gets audited

- **Protocol adherence** — were SOC protocols followed where applicable? Where deviated, was the deviation documented?
- **Coding accuracy** — did the chart support the billed code? (Connects to [[charge-capture]] and [[dental-grade-3-undercoded-by-22-percent]].)
- **Anesthesia compliance** — was the [[anesthesia-tier-policy]] followed?
- **Controlled substance log integrity** — does the chart document match the controlled-substance log?
- **Documentation quality** — is the chart legible, complete, properly signed?

## Sample size

- Tier A: 25 charts per quarter per clinic
- Tier B: 15 charts per quarter per clinic
- Tier C: 10 charts per quarter per clinic
- Stratified by service line (GP / dental / surgical / urgent care)

## Outputs

- **Per-clinic scorecard** — share of charts meeting standard, by category
- **Region-level pattern** — are there systemic issues clustering in certain clinics?
- **Protocol-library feedback** — are there protocols that DVMs deviate from consistently? That's a signal the protocol may need revision.

## Cadence

- Audits done by the RMD or designee throughout the quarter
- Results reviewed at the [[2026-04-15-monthly-clinical-council]]
- Annual rollup to the board's Clinical Quality committee (chaired informally by [[dr-amelia-foster]])

## Why this is high-leverage

[[nps-correlates-with-medical-director-engagement]] showed that clinics whose RMD did monthly chart rounds posted +18 NPS points. The quarterly audit is the floor; the monthly chart-rounds at flagships is the aspiration. The audit catches the things the rounds miss.
