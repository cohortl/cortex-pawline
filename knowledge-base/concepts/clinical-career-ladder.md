# Clinical career ladder

The published progression for DVMs at Pawline: Associate → Senior Associate → Medical Director → Regional Medical Director (and parallel academic / specialty tracks). The ladder gives DVMs a visible path that's specific to the multi-clinic operating environment.

## The rungs

### Associate DVM (entry — year 0-2)

- New-grad or early-career
- Standard ProSal compensation
- Mentorship pairing with a senior associate or MD
- 90-day onboarding per [[new-dvm-onboarding-playbook]]

### Senior Associate DVM (year 2-5)

- Earned by tenure + sustained ADR + clinical-quality-review pass rate
- Higher base in the ProSal split
- Mentorship of incoming associates
- Eligibility for [[equity-participation-program]] starts here

### Medical Director (clinic-level)

- Earned, not tenure-based — requires demonstrated clinical leadership
- Owns clinical quality at one clinic
- Compensation: hybrid base + production share + admin time allocation
- Equity grant size step up

### Regional Medical Director (regional-level)

- Half of the [[medical-director-dyad]]
- Owns clinical quality across 8-15 clinics
- Compensation: salary + equity-heavy
- Direct line to CMO

### Parallel tracks

- **Specialist track** — DVMs pursuing board certification (cardiology, internal medicine, surgery, etc.) follow a different progression: residency support, specialty production model, attending DVM at flagship clinics
- **Academic / teaching track** — for DVMs running clinical training, residency mentorship, or external CE; less formal at this stage

## Why the ladder matters

The strongest signal for DVM retention is "I can see my next step here," per [[dvm-retention-decision-driver-autonomy]]. The autonomy finding tells us about *day-to-day*; the ladder tells us about *trajectory*. Both have to work.

## Promotion mechanics

- **Self-nomination + RMD endorsement** for Senior Associate
- **RMD nomination + CMO sign-off** for Medical Director
- **CMO + COO joint nomination + CEO sign-off** for Regional Medical Director
- Annual promotion cycle in Q1, with off-cycle for exceptional cases

## Where it strains

- **Bottleneck at the MD level**: only ~30 MD seats across 80 clinics; tenure-track DVMs sometimes wait longer than they want
- **Geography**: an MD-ready DVM in a region without an opening can't promote without relocating
- **Specialty seats**: only ~12 specialist DVMs across the company; specialty-track ambitions exceed supply of seats
