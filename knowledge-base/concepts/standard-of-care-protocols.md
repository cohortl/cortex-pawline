# Standard of care (SOC) protocols

Pawline's clinical protocol library — the set of evidence-based clinical pathways the company expects DVMs to follow for common presentations and procedures. Currently at v2 ([[2026-02-18-clinical-protocol-v2-publish]]), spans ~140 protocols across preventive, sick-visit, surgical, and dental domains.

## What's in scope

- Preventive care (vaccination schedules, parasite prevention, wellness exams)
- Common sick-visit presentations (vomiting, diarrhea, lameness, otitis)
- Routine surgical pathways (spay, neuter, mass removal, dental extraction)
- Dental grading and treatment branching (see [[dental-grading-system]])
- Anesthesia (see [[anesthesia-tier-policy]])
- Pain management
- Critical-case triage (see [[triage-acuity-levels]])

## What's NOT in scope

Anything where DVM judgment exceeds the protocol value. Specialty cases, complex surgical pathways, and atypical presentations stay in DVM discretion. The protocol library is a floor, not a ceiling.

## Compliance vs. discretion

Per [[clinical-protocols-policy]]:
- **Where SOC applies**: DVM must either follow it or document the deviation in the chart with reasoning
- **Where SOC doesn't apply**: DVM judgment, no documentation required beyond standard charting
- **Coding**: protocol-aligned coding is required for billing accuracy ([[charge-capture]] and [[dental-grade-3-undercoded-by-22-percent]] both connect here)

## Why this matters at scale

Without SOC, 80 clinics produce 80 different versions of "appropriate care." Quality varies. Coding varies. Outcomes can't be benchmarked. The library is what makes clinical-quality KPIs meaningful across the company.
