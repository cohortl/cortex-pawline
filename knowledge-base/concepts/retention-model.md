# Retention model

Pawline's framework for understanding what keeps DVMs (and to a lesser degree, technicians and practice managers) at Pawline — and what causes them to leave. Built from exit-interview data, stay-interview data, and longitudinal cohort tracking.

## DVM retention drivers (in order)

Per the 2025 exit-interview analysis ([[dvm-retention-decision-driver-autonomy]]):

1. **Clinical autonomy** — being trusted to make calls within the SOC envelope without micromanagement
2. **Clear next step** — a visible career path ([[clinical-career-ladder]])
3. **Quality of clinical-team peers** — strong peers, especially senior MDs and RMDs, retain DVMs even when comp lags
4. **Compensation total** — on the order of #4-5, not #1
5. **Schedule sustainability** — genuine work-life support, especially around on-call and weekend coverage
6. **Equity participation** — important for those eligible, neutral for those not yet
7. **Clinic culture** — the practice manager + technician quality

## What we measure

- **Annual DVM retention** (rolling): KPI #12 in [[clinical-kpi-definitions]]; current 89.4%
- **First-year retention** (new associates): 84.5% (lower; on-ramp friction)
- **Critical-talent retention** (Senior Associate and above): 93.1%
- **Departure reasons** (from exit interviews, structured)
- **Cohort longitudinal**: how does retention look 1, 2, 3 years post-acquisition for acquired-clinic DVMs

## What we do about it

- **Stay interviews** at 90 days and annually thereafter for all DVMs Senior Associate and above
- **Mentorship pairing** — see [[new-dvm-onboarding-playbook]]
- **Schedule guardrails** — on-call rotation discipline; minimum coverage standards
- **Clinical autonomy floor** — the [[clinical-protocols-policy]] explicitly preserves DVM discretion above the SOC layer
- **Equity refresh cycle** — annual refresh reinforces "you are wanted here long-term"

## Cohort observation: acquired-clinic DVM retention

DVMs at acquired clinics retain at slightly lower rates than DVMs at organic clinics over the first 24 months (84% vs 89%). After 24 months, rates converge. The first-24-month gap is concentrated in the first 6 months — when integration friction is highest. The [[acquisition-integration-playbook]] T+30 / T+90 / T+180 milestones include explicit DVM-retention checkpoints.

## What we don't measure well yet

- Technician retention (data quality is poorer; ADP records are the primary source)
- "Almost-quit" near-misses (DVMs who considered leaving but stayed — captured in stay interviews but unevenly)
