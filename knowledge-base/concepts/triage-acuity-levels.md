# Triage acuity levels

The intake classification used at every Pawline clinic to decide whether a presenting patient is seen now (red), within an hour (yellow), or routed to the schedule (green). Adapted from human emergency triage systems.

## The levels

- **Red — Critical.** Life-threatening: respiratory distress, severe trauma, suspected GDV (bloat), seizure cluster, blocked cat, dystocia, severe bleeding, anaphylaxis. Action: stop normal flow, take to treatment area immediately, alert DVM. If the clinic doesn't have ER capability, simultaneously call ahead to the regional flagship or external ER.
- **Yellow — Urgent.** Needs same-day care but not immediately life-threatening: lameness, vomiting/diarrhea persisting >24h, otitis, lacerations, suspected UTI, mild allergic reactions. Action: work into the day's schedule within 1-2 hours.
- **Green — Routine.** Standard sick visit or wellness: routine vaccines, mild GI without dehydration, behavioral concerns, dermatology. Action: schedule normally; same-day if capacity, next-business-day otherwise.

## Who triages

- Initial triage by client-services staff at the front desk based on a structured intake script
- Re-triage by the credentialed technician at vitals
- DVM is the final authority on level — can up- or down-triage at any point

## Why it's standardized

A consistent triage system across 80 clinics produces three goods:
- **Clinical safety**: red cases are never delayed by inadvertent green-track scheduling
- **Cross-clinic referral**: when a Tier C clinic phones a Tier A flagship, "we're sending a yellow" is a shared protocol
- **Capacity planning**: the daily mix of red/yellow/green at a clinic is a leading indicator for staffing and overflow

## Limitations

- Front-desk triage is the weakest link; ongoing training is part of [[new-dvm-onboarding-playbook]] and the staff onboarding cycle
- Telephone triage of red cases is hard; we err toward "come in now" rather than telephone advice
