# Controlled Substances Policy

Pawline's policy on DEA Schedule II-V drugs. Compliance is non-negotiable; loss of DEA registration at a clinic ripples to network risk profile. Owner: CMO + Director of Clinical Quality. Audience: every DVM, every credentialed technician, every Practice Manager.

## DEA registration

- Each clinic handling controlled substances has its own DEA registration
- Registration is per-clinic, per-DVM-of-record where applicable
- Acquired clinics: DEA transfer / new application managed in [[acquisition-integration-playbook]] T-day milestone
- New clinics: DEA registration is a pre-opening prerequisite

## Storage

- All Schedule II drugs in a securely-locked, anchored cabinet or safe
- Schedule III-V in locked storage (safe is preferred but lockable cabinet permitted)
- Two-key access control where possible
- Storage location accessible only to credentialed personnel

## Logging

### Schedule II

- Daily inventory count, recorded in Cornerstone (or legacy log for non-Cornerstone clinics)
- Two-person verification for every dose drawn from a Schedule II vial
- Reconciliation against orders + administration + dispensing daily

### Schedule III-V

- Biennial federal inventory count (DEA requirement)
- Pawline-internal quarterly counts (closer than federal minimum)
- Two-person verification for each dose drawn

## Discrepancies

- Discrepancy = physical count diverges from log without same-day documented explanation
- Investigation begins within 4 hours of discovery
- Resolution within 24 hours expected
- Unresolved at 72 hours: Director of Clinical Quality notified
- Pattern (3+ discrepancies in 90 days at one clinic): triggered review per [[quality-of-care-review-playbook]]

Network discrepancy rate target: <0.1% ([[controlled-substance-discrepancy-rate-target]]). Current rate: 0.07%.

## Audit cadence

- **Internal monthly**: Practice Manager self-audit
- **Internal quarterly**: ROD audit with sampled charts
- **Annual third-party**: rotated among regions
- **DEA inspections**: annual at flagship clinics, episodic elsewhere

## Personnel requirements

- Only DEA-registered DVMs can prescribe controlled substances
- Only credentialed technicians can administer (under DVM oversight)
- Annual training on DEA compliance for every clinic-side staff member
- Annual training on this policy for new hires within 30 days of start

## Reporting + breach response

- Theft, loss, or unexplained shortfall: report to DEA per Form 106 within 1 business day
- Internal escalation: Practice Manager → ROD → Director of Clinical Quality → CMO
- Crisis-comms protocol per [[crisis-communications-playbook]] for material events

## What we don't do

- Don't dispense controlled substances without complete prescriptive paperwork
- Don't take shortcuts on the two-person verification, ever
- Don't store controlled substances outside designated secure storage
- Don't transport between clinics except via DEA-permitted procedures
