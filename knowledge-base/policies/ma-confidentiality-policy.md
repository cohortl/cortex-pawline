# M&A Confidentiality Policy

How Pawline handles confidentiality through the M&A pipeline — sourcing, conversations, NDA, LOI, diligence, signing, and pre-close. Audience: corp dev team + diligence team + integration team. Owner: Head of M&A + General Counsel.

## NDA discipline

- All target conversations beyond initial introduction are NDA-gated
- Pawline's standard NDA template is used by default; deviations require General Counsel review
- NDAs are mutual where possible (we share information about Pawline; sellers share about themselves)
- NDA breaches are taken seriously — career consequence territory

## Information access

### Pre-NDA

- Only generic Pawline information (publicly-available + brand materials)
- No financial detail shared in either direction

### Post-NDA, pre-LOI

- Seller financials shared with corp dev + CFO + analyst staff
- Limited inside Pawline: M&A team + CFO + CEO only
- No clinic-level disclosure to operations or clinical teams

### Post-LOI, pre-close

- Diligence team gains access (operations, clinical-quality reviewer, IT)
- Acquired-clinic-specific information shared with the assigned RMD/ROD pair only when needed for post-close planning
- Still confidential within Pawline

### At close (T-day)

- Internal staff announcement timed per the [[acquisition-integration-playbook]] communication plan
- External announcement (clients, vendors, etc.) per the comms plan

## Target-tracking confidentiality

The active M&A pipeline (in `internal/ma-pipeline/`) is exec/board-only. Specifically:

- Named targets in active conversations: never visible to all-staff
- Named targets in early sourcing: never visible to all-staff
- Aggregate pipeline counts and geographic mix: visible to all-staff (per [[ma-pipeline-2026-q2]])

## What's allowed in conversation

- Talking about acquisitions in general terms
- Talking about closed acquisitions (post-close announcement)
- Talking about Pawline's [[acquisition-criteria]] and [[ebitda-add-backs-policy]] in general terms
- Discussing closed acquisition cohort patterns (e.g., [[acquired-cohort-2024-outperforming-2023]])

## What's not allowed in conversation

- Naming specific targets in active conversations
- Speculating on competing bids
- Sharing seller-financial information outside the diligence team
- Discussing valuation negotiations in specific terms

## Pre-close communication discipline

- No premature seller-side communication to seller's staff (sellers occasionally pre-leak; Pawline does not encourage this)
- Pawline-side staff awareness on a need-to-know basis only
- Public-facing communication coordinated with seller's counsel through close

## Breaches

- Internal breach (Pawline employee disclosing): managed under employment-policy framework; consequences scaled to severity
- External breach (broker, advisor, third party): contractual remedies + relationship review
- Material breach affecting close: General Counsel + CEO immediately notified

## Why this policy exists

Vet-services M&A is a relationship business. Reputation for discretion is part of why sellers come to Pawline rather than larger consolidators. Sloppy talk about pipeline is the fastest way to lose that reputation.
