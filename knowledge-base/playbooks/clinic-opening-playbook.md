# Clinic Opening Playbook (Greenfield)

How Pawline opens a new clinic from scratch — site selection through grand opening. Used rarely (most growth is acquisition); applied when a strategic geography requires a greenfield because no acquirable target exists, or when a flagship cluster needs explicit anchor capacity.

## The 6-month timeline

### Month -6 to -4 — Site selection

- Cluster-fit confirmed (per [[regional-cluster-economics]])
- Demographics, density, and competitor analysis
- 3-5 candidate locations evaluated
- Lease negotiation (or buy if Pawline is in real-estate-acquiring posture)
- Final site selected

### Month -4 to -2 — Build-out

- Architectural plans
- Permits + DEA registration application
- Equipment procurement (per [[2025-04-15-centralize-surgical-procurement]] central catalog)
- IT infrastructure (Cornerstone tenant, network, security)
- Tier classification confirmed (likely Tier B for greenfield; Tier A only if explicit flagship strategy)

### Month -2 to -1 — Hiring + soft setup

- Practice Manager hired (T-2 months)
- Lead DVM hired (T-2 months)
- Technician team hired (T-1 month)
- Client-services staff hired (T-3 weeks)
- Pre-opening staff training (Pawline orientation, SOC protocols, Cornerstone)
- Soft-opening dry run

### Month 0 — Grand opening

- Public opening
- Marketing campaign (digital + community)
- First 30 days: aggressive new-client acquisition
- Daily ops support from regional dyad
- DVM coverage from regional relief pool for surge days

### Month +1 to +6 — Ramp

- New-client acquisition focus
- Building patient panel
- Steady increase in daily visit volume
- Margin profile catches up to standard Tier B by month ~9-12

## What greenfield costs

- Build-out: $400-700K (Tier B), $1.2-2M (Tier A)
- Equipment: $250-400K (Tier B basic), $800K-1.4M (Tier A with surgical suite)
- Pre-opening payroll: $80-120K (3-month ramp)
- Marketing launch: $30-50K
- Total Tier B: ~$800K-1.2M; Total Tier A: ~$2.3M-3.8M

## When greenfield beats acquisition

- No acquirable target exists in the geography
- The cluster needs an anchor specifically positioned (e.g., the flagship in a developing metro)
- Cost-of-acquisition multiples are at peaks (per [[competitor-acquisition-multiples-rising]])

## When acquisition beats greenfield

- Existing patient panel + DVM team already in place at the target
- The target's location is already optimal
- Speed to revenue matters

## Common pitfalls

- Optimistic ramp assumptions — Tier B greenfield typically takes 9-12 months to break even, not 6
- Underestimating community-trust cold-start — opening in a neighborhood without word-of-mouth is genuinely harder than acquiring the local goodwill
- DVM hiring lead time — strong DVMs need 4-6 month notice periods at their current employers
