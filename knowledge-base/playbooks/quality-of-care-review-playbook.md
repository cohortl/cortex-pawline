# Quality of Care Review Playbook

How Pawline conducts the quarterly chart-audit + peer-review process. The operational procedure behind [[clinical-quality-review]]. Owned by the CMO; executed by RMDs and the Director of Clinical Quality.

## Cadence

- **Quarterly**: every clinic gets a chart audit. Sample size scales with clinic tier (Tier A: 25, Tier B: 15, Tier C: 10).
- **Bi-annual**: peer-review session at the regional clinical council
- **Ad-hoc**: triggered review for incidents, pattern concerns, or DEA / regulatory events

## Quarterly chart-audit procedure

### Sampling

- Stratified by service line (GP / dental / surgical / urgent care)
- Random within strata
- Drawn from previous-quarter visits
- Includes a deliberate sample of newly-acquired-clinic charts (per [[acquisition-integration-playbook]] T+30 / T+90 milestones)

### What's checked

- **Protocol adherence** — was [[standard-of-care-protocols]] followed where applicable? Where deviated, was the deviation documented?
- **Coding accuracy** — did the chart support the billed code?
- **Anesthesia compliance** — was the [[anesthesia-tier-policy]] followed?
- **Controlled substance log integrity** — does the chart match the log?
- **Documentation quality** — legible, complete, signed

### Conducted by

- RMD or RMD designee (typically a senior MD in the region)
- 2-4 hours per clinic per quarter
- Documented in a structured Cornerstone form

### Output

- **Per-clinic scorecard** — share of charts meeting standard, by category
- **Region-level pattern** — clusters of similar issues
- **Protocol-library feedback** — DVMs deviating consistently from a protocol may signal protocol revision needed

## Peer review (bi-annual)

- 1-2 cases per DVM presented to the regional clinical council
- Cases selected for educational value (interesting differential, novel presentation, clinical-quality lessons) — NOT only "problem cases"
- Confidential to the council; not used for performance evaluation directly

## Triggered review

- Anesthesia incident with adverse outcome
- Controlled-substance discrepancy unresolved within 72 hours
- Repeat protocol-deviation pattern at a clinic
- DEA / regulatory event
- Significant client complaint with clinical merit

Triggered reviews involve the Director of Clinical Quality + RMD + (where appropriate) external clinical specialist consultation.

## Outcomes

- **Most common**: feedback to clinic + RMD-coaching; no formal action
- **Pattern concerns**: focused intervention plan for the clinic, 60-day follow-up
- **Serious concerns**: escalation to CMO; in extreme cases, performance-improvement plan or termination

## Why this works

- Quarterly cadence matches the natural operating rhythm
- Sample sizes are large enough to catch patterns but small enough to actually do
- Stratified sampling means surgical and dental work get audited regularly even though they're a minority of visits
