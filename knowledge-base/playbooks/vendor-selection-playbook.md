# Vendor Selection Playbook

The operational procedure for evaluating, contracting, and onboarding new vendors at Pawline. Used by anyone leading a vendor-selection process — from corporate IT to clinical-supplies. Owner: COO + Head of Procurement (rolled into COO function).

## When to use this playbook

- Adding a new vendor to the central catalog
- Replacing an existing strategic-tier vendor
- Adding a new connector / integration ([[connectors]])
- Any contract category over $25K annual

## The four phases

### 1. Sourcing

- Define the requirement clearly — what problem does this vendor solve? Who is the operational owner?
- Identify 3-5 candidate vendors (broker referrals, industry research, peer rec)
- Issue brief RFI to candidates
- Shortlist to 2-3 for full evaluation

### 2. Evaluation

- Structured RFP with consistent requirements across vendors
- Reference checks with at least 2 customers per vendor
- Demo / pilot if applicable
- Total cost of ownership modeling (not just sticker price)
- Cultural fit assessment for strategic / long-term relationships

### 3. Contracting

- Pricing negotiation
- SLA / service-level commitments
- Termination clause review
- Data-handling clause review (especially for vendors touching client / patient data)
- Legal review (outside fractional counsel for most contracts)
- COO approval for >$25K annual; CEO sign-off for strategic-tier

### 4. Onboarding

- Implementation plan with named owner
- Communication plan to affected clinics / regions
- Performance review cadence established at outset
- Reference baseline metrics

## Selection criteria (in order)

1. **Clinical / operational fit** — does this vendor actually do the thing we need?
2. **Pricing** — TCO not sticker price
3. **Service quality + responsiveness** — SLAs in writing, references confirmed
4. **Financial stability** — for strategic vendors only; we don't want to be left holding a contract when a vendor folds
5. **Cultural fit** — for long-term partnerships specifically

## What this playbook saves you from

- The "everyone uses them" trap (industry default isn't always right for Pawline scale)
- Multi-year auto-renewal lock-in
- Sticker-price decisions that create TCO disasters

## Common pitfalls

- Skipping reference checks because the demo was good
- Negotiating pricing without negotiating SLAs
- Contracting without an explicit operational owner
- Auto-renewing without the contractual review
