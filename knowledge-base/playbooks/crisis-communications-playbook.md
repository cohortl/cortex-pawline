# Crisis Communications Playbook

How Pawline communicates in a clinical or operational crisis. Used for: clinical incidents with adverse outcomes, controlled-substance incidents requiring DEA notification, data-security events, public-relations situations, multi-clinic operational disruptions (regulatory, weather, infrastructure). Owner: COO + CEO + CMO + General Counsel (fractional).

## Crisis tiers

### Tier 1 — Internal-only

- Single-clinic operational disruption with no public visibility
- Internal-only clinical pattern concern
- Single-vendor failure with limited downstream impact
- **Comms scope**: clinic + region + relevant exec function

### Tier 2 — Limited external

- Clinical incident with single-client family communication
- Single-clinic DEA / regulatory event
- Localized data-security event affecting one clinic's clients
- **Comms scope**: affected client(s) + clinic + region + exec team + legal

### Tier 3 — Multi-stakeholder

- Multi-clinic clinical pattern concern
- Multi-clinic DEA / regulatory event
- Multi-clinic data-security event
- Public-facing PR concerns
- **Comms scope**: full exec team + board + legal + PR (external) + affected clients + staff

## Standing principles

- **Patient/client first** — the affected family hears it first, before any other communication
- **Honesty over comfort** — don't sugarcoat; don't speculate beyond facts
- **One spokesperson** — designated per crisis; nobody else speaks publicly
- **Document continuously** — running log maintained by an assigned crisis-recorder
- **Legal in the room** — for Tier 2+, fractional GC engaged from minute one

## Tier 1 procedure

1. Practice manager + RMD informed within 4 hours of event
2. ROD informed within 24 hours
3. Internal log started
4. Resolution + post-incident review within 7 days

## Tier 2 procedure

1. Practice manager + RMD informed immediately (within 1 hour)
2. Affected client family contacted within 4-12 hours (case-dependent)
3. CMO + COO + General Counsel informed within 6 hours
4. Crisis recorder assigned
5. CEO informed within 24 hours
6. Post-incident review within 14 days
7. Protocol-library implications considered ([[standard-of-care-protocols]])

## Tier 3 procedure

1. CEO + CMO + COO + General Counsel notified immediately
2. Spokesperson designated (typically CEO or CMO)
3. Crisis room established (physical or virtual)
4. Board notified within 24 hours
5. PR firm engaged if public-facing
6. Daily situation reports until resolved
7. Comprehensive post-incident review within 30 days

## What we don't do

- Never speculate publicly about cause before facts are confirmed
- Never blame staff publicly even when staff actions contributed
- Never promise outcomes we can't deliver (legal exposure)

## Pre-crisis prep

- General Counsel relationship maintained year-round
- PR firm on retainer (Tier 3 readiness only)
- Practice managers complete annual crisis-comms briefing
- This playbook reviewed annually by COO + CMO + General Counsel
