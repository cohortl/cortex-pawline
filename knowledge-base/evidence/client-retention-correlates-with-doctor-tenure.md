# Client retention correlates with doctor tenure

24-month client-retention rate increases ~4 percentage points for every additional year of attending DVM tenure, controlled for clinic tier, region, and species mix. A 1-year DVM's panel retains at ~71%; a 5-year DVM's panel retains at ~87%. The slope flattens after ~6-7 years (asymptote around 89%). Methodology: longitudinal cohort tracking via Cornerstone visit-chain analysis. Implication: DVM retention is a clinical-quality and revenue lever simultaneously — the retention story isn't just an HR concern. Reinforces the strategic case for [[equity-participation-program]] and the [[clinical-career-ladder]] visible-progression argument; supports [[2026-04-25-comp-restructure-2027]]'s focus on long-tenure DVM economics.
