# Competitor acquisition multiples rising

Strategic acquirers in vet-services (Mars-owned platforms, PetVet Care Centers, NVA, Thrive Pet Healthcare) have been paying 9-11x adjusted EBITDA in 2025-2026 mid-market deals (clinics in the $3-12M revenue band) — up from 7-8x in 2023. Sources: broker conversations, deal-tracking newsletters, public PE secondary-data, anecdotal LOI competition reports. Confidence is medium because precise comparable data is hard to triangulate (private deals, varying definitions of EBITDA). Implication for [[acquisition-criteria]]: targets willing to take Pawline-multiples (5.5-9x with rollover) are a self-selecting cohort — typically valuing cultural fit over headline price. The trend is partial driver of [[texas-acquisition-pipeline-thinning]] and a concern flagged at [[2026-q1-board-meeting]].
