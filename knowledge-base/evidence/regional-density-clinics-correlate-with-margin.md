# Regional density correlates with margin

Regions with >12 clinics show 4-6 percentage points of margin uplift over regions with <8 clinics, controlled for clinic tier, region cost-of-living, and acquisition cohort. The break-even threshold appears around 8-9 clinics — below which fixed regional overhead (RMD, ROD, area ops manager, lab logistics) eats the margin advantage of operating at any scale. Above 15 clinics, the marginal density premium flattens (you stop adding new shared services). Implication: M&A scoring should weight cluster-fit heavily; isolated targets in low-density geographies fail this evidence. This finding is the quantitative anchor for [[acquisition-criteria]] geographic scoring.
