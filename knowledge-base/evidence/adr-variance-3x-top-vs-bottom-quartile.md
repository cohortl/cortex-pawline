# ADR variance — 3x top vs bottom quartile

Across 280+ DVMs at Pawline, ADR (per shift) variance between top-quartile and bottom-quartile is 3.0x — top quartile averaging $5,400/shift, bottom quartile averaging $1,800/shift. Controlled for tenure (>2 years), region, and clinic tier; these are persistent rather than sample-noise differences. Drivers: surgical case mix, charge-capture discipline, dental-grading accuracy, panel size. Variance is the headline argument for [[2026-04-25-comp-restructure-2027]] — straight ProSal amplifies the variance because base draws are lower for low-ADR DVMs, which depresses retention of doctors who could improve with mentorship.
