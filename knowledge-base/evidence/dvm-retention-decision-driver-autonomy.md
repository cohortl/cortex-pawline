# DVM retention — autonomy is the #1 driver

2025 exit-interview analysis (n=42 departing DVMs) found "clinical autonomy" cited as the top reason in 38% of cases — well above compensation (22%), schedule (18%), or career path (12%). The autonomy theme breaks into two sub-categories: (1) protocol over-prescription — DVMs feeling SOC protocols leave too little judgment, addressed in [[clinical-protocols-policy]]; (2) management micromanagement — addressed by the [[medical-director-dyad]] model. Implication: comp changes alone won't move retention; protecting clinical autonomy in [[standard-of-care-protocols]] design and in management practice is structurally important. This finding shaped both [[2026-02-18-clinical-protocol-v2-publish]] (clearer "where SOC applies vs DVM discretion" boundary) and [[2026-04-25-comp-restructure-2027]] (less production pressure releases more autonomy in case selection).
