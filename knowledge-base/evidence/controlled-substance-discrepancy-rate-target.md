# Controlled-substance discrepancy rate — target met

Pawline's published internal target for controlled-substance discrepancy rate is <0.1% (defined as: percentage of controlled-substance counts where the physical count diverges from the log without a same-day documented explanation). Q1 2026 measured rate: 0.07% — below target. Year-over-year: down from 0.14% in 2024 and 0.09% in 2025. Drivers of the improvement: (1) standardization on Cornerstone (legacy PIMS were the source of most discrepancy noise); (2) tightened two-person-verification protocol, embedded in [[2026-02-18-clinical-protocol-v2-publish]]; (3) monthly self-audit cadence at the clinic level, formalized in 2024. Watch: a single clinic with elevated discrepancies skews the network number; we monitor by-clinic, not just consolidated.
