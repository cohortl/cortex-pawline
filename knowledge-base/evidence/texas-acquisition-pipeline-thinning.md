# Texas acquisition pipeline thinning

Pawline's 2026 Q1 pipeline review identified ~40% fewer high-quality TX targets at our preferred size band ($3-8M revenue, cluster-fit) than 2025's pipeline. Drivers (qualitative, from broker conversations and pipeline tracking): (1) larger consolidators (notably Mars-owned and PetVet Care Centers) have been more aggressive in TX in 2025-2026; (2) the few sellable independents have been bid up to multiples that fail [[acquisition-criteria]] thresholds — see [[competitor-acquisition-multiples-rising]]; (3) the geography of Pawline's existing TX footprint (Dallas-Fort Worth + Houston) leaves Austin / San Antonio as the next cluster-builder targets, and the available targets in those metros have been sparse. Implication: TX growth in 2026 may be slower than plan; Mountain or West infill may be the better deployment focus.
